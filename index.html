Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President and Beloved Scientist

Here's a time line of Dr. Apj Abdul Kalam:

  • 1931- Born in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954- Graduate in Physics from saint joseph's college, Tiruchirapalli. he moves to madras to pursue further education.
  • 1960- After completing education in Aeronautical engineering from madras institute of technology(MIT), kalam join DRDO as Scientist
  • 1969- Join ISRO(Indian Space Research Organization) where he helms india's first Satellite Launch Vehicle(SLV-III)
  • 1980- Lead india's entry into space club by putting Rohini Satellite into earth's orbit with the first indigenous SLV-III
  • 1980-1990- Develops Prithvi and Agni Missiles as chief of the integrated guided missile development programme, he is credited with the development and organization of Agni and Prithvi Missile
  • 1981- Receives Padma Bhushan for contribution to the field of science and technology
  • 1990- Honoured with Padma Vibhushan, India's Second highest civilian honour
  • 1992-1999- Appointed as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and secretary of the DRDO
  • 1997- Honoured with Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award for his contribution in science and technology and modification of defence technology in india
  • 1998- Spearhead the Pokhran mission, India conduct Pokharan II nuclear test with kalam being one of the forces behind its success
  • 1999-2001- Serve as Chief Scientific Adviser to Government of India
  • 2002-2007- Elected as India's 11th President, During his tenure he was popularly known as People's President
  • 2012- Launches 'What I Can Give Movement' The programme for indian youth aimed at defeating corruption
  • 2015- Died at Shillong. at age of 83, kalam passes away due to cardiac arrest while delivering lecture at IIM Shillong
    • To succeed in life and achieve results, you must understand and master three mighty forces - desire, belief and expectation

    -- Dr Apj Abdul Kalam
